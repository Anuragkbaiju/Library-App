In "Intermezzo," Sally Rooney presents an exquisitely moving story about grief, love, and family. The novel centers on brothers Peter and Ivan Koubek, who, despite their shared lineage, lead markedly different lives. Peter is an attractive, successful lawyer, while Ivan is a socially awkward, intelligent chess genius. Following the death of their father, both brothers grapple with their own complex relationships, each marked by significant age differences. Rooney delves into themes of grief, love, and family, offering a nuanced exploration of human connections and the intricacies of sibling dynamics.
