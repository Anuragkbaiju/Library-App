In "The Women," Kristin Hannah transports readers to 1965 California, where 20-year-old nursing student Frances "Frankie" McGrath's life takes an unexpected turn. Inspired by the notion that "women can be heroes, too," Frankie joins the Army Nurse Corps to follow her brother to Vietnam. There, she confronts the brutal realities of war, forming deep bonds with fellow nurses amidst the chaos. Upon returning home, Frankie faces a divided America, where the sacrifices of female veterans are often overlooked. Hannah's evocative storytelling sheds light on the untold stories of these courageous women, offering a poignant exploration of heroism, friendship, and the enduring impacts of war.
