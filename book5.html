In "The Anxious Generation: How the Great Rewiring of Childhood Is Causing an Epidemic of Mental Illness," social psychologist Jonathan Haidt examines the alarming rise in adolescent mental health issues coinciding with the proliferation of smartphones and social media. Haidt traces the decline of "play-based" childhoods, beginning in the 1980s, and the subsequent emergence of "phone-based" childhoods in the early 2010s. He argues that this shift has led to increased rates of anxiety, depression, and self-harm among teenagers, particularly adolescent girls. The book delves into how reduced opportunities for independent play and exploration have hindered the development of resilience in children. Haidt also offers actionable solutions for parents, educators, and policymakers to foster healthier childhoods and mitigate the mental health crisis affecting today's youth.
